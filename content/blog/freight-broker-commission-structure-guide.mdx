# The Complete Guide to Freight Broker Commission Structures

**Understanding commission structures is the key to maximizing your freight broker income.** This guide reveals exactly how commissions work and how to negotiate the best deal.

## Commission Models Explained

### 1. Straight Commission (Most Common)
**Structure**: 25-60% of gross profit
**Best for**: Experienced brokers with established books

**Example Calculation**:
- Load rate: $3,000
- Carrier pay: $2,400
- Gross profit: $600
- Your commission (40%): $240

**Monthly Reality Check**:
- 50 loads × $240 = $12,000/month
- Annual: $144,000

### 2. Base + Commission
**Structure**: $35,000-$50,000 base + 15-25% commission
**Best for**: New brokers wanting stability

**Typical Package**:
- Base: $40,000/year
- Commission: 20% of gross profit
- Average total: $65,000-$85,000

### 3. Tiered Commission
**Structure**: Commission rate increases with performance

**Common Tiers**:
- $0-$10,000 GP: 25%
- $10,001-$25,000: 35%
- $25,001-$50,000: 45%
- $50,000+: 50-60%

### 4. Draw Against Commission
**Structure**: Guaranteed monthly draw, reconciled quarterly
**Example**: $4,000/month draw against 40% commission

## Commission Negotiation Strategies

### What You Can Negotiate:
1. **Commission percentage** (most important)
2. **Tier thresholds** (when rates increase)
3. **House account access**
4. **Expense reimbursements**
5. **Bonus structures**

### Leverage Points:
- Previous gross profit generated
- Customer relationships you bring
- Specialized knowledge (hazmat, oversized)
- Competing offers

## Red Flags in Commission Structures

⚠️ **Warning Signs**:
- Commission caps (limits earning potential)
- Charge-backs for carrier issues
- Unclear calculation methods
- No written agreement
- Delayed payment terms

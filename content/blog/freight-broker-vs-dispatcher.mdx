# Freight Broker vs Dispatcher: Complete Comparison Guide

**Many people confuse freight brokers with dispatchers, but they're completely different roles.** This comprehensive guide explains the key differences, helping you choose the right career path.

## Quick Comparison Overview

| Aspect | Freight Broker | Dispatcher |
|--------|---------------|------------|
| **Works For** | Shippers | Carriers/Drivers |
| **Main Role** | Find loads for carriers | Find carriers for loads |
| **License Required** | Yes (MC Number) | No |
| **Average Salary** | $65,000-$150,000 | $45,000-$75,000 |
| **Commission** | 25-60% of profit | Usually hourly/salary |
| **Startup Cost** | $5,000-$10,000 | $500-$1,000 |

## What is a Freight Broker?

### Definition
A freight broker is an intermediary who connects shippers needing to transport goods with carriers who can move those goods. They never take possession of freight.

### Primary Responsibilities
- Find and negotiate with shippers
- Source reliable carriers
- Negotiate rates both ways
- Ensure proper documentation
- Handle claims and issues
- Build long-term relationships

### Who They Work For
- Independent (own authority)
- Brokerage companies
- 3PL companies
- Themselves as agents

### Daily Activities
- Cold calling shippers
- Negotiating rates
- Posting loads
- Vetting carriers
- Tracking shipments
- Problem resolution

## What is a Dispatcher?

### Definition
A dispatcher works directly for trucking companies or owner-operators, managing their daily operations and finding profitable loads to haul.

### Primary Responsibilities
- Find loads for specific trucks
- Plan efficient routes
- Manage driver schedules
- Communicate with brokers
- Handle paperwork
- Coordinate maintenance

### Who They Work For
- Trucking companies
- Owner-operators
- Fleet owners
- Independent contractors

### Daily Activities
- Searching load boards
- Negotiating with brokers
- Route planning
- Driver communication
- Document management
- Tracking deliveries

## Key Differences Explained

### 1. Client Relationship

**Freight Broker**:
- Serves shippers primarily
- Builds shipper relationships
- Responsible to shipper
- Paid by shipper

**Dispatcher**:
- Serves carriers/drivers
- Builds broker relationships
- Responsible to carrier
- Paid by carrier

### 2. Revenue Model

**Freight Broker Income**:
```
Shipper Rate: $3,000
Carrier Rate: $2,500
Broker Profit: $500
Commission (40%): $200
```

**Dispatcher Income**:
```
Hourly: $20-$35/hour
Or
Per Load: $50-$100
Or
Percentage: 3-7% of gross
```

### 3. Licensing Requirements

**Freight Broker**:
- MC Number required ($300)
- $75,000 surety bond
- Process agents (BOC-3)
- Business entity

**Dispatcher**:
- No license required
- No bond needed
- Work from anywhere
- Minimal requirements

### 4. Startup Investment

**Freight Broker Costs**:
- License and bond: $1,500-$10,000
- Software: $200-$500/month
- Marketing: $500-$2,000/month
- Total: $5,000-$15,000

**Dispatcher Costs**:
- Computer: $500-$1,000
- Phone: $50/month
- Load boards: $50-$200/month
- Total: $500-$1,500

## Salary Comparison

### Freight Broker Earnings

**Entry Level (Year 1)**:
- Base: $35,000-$45,000
- Commission: $10,000-$20,000
- Total: $45,000-$65,000

**Experienced (3-5 Years)**:
- Base: $50,000-$60,000
- Commission: $40,000-$90,000
- Total: $90,000-$150,000

**Top Performers**:
- Commission only: $200,000-$500,000
- Own authority: Unlimited

### Dispatcher Earnings

**Entry Level**:
- Hourly: $15-$20 ($31,000-$42,000)
- Limited growth potential

**Experienced**:
- Hourly: $25-$35 ($52,000-$73,000)
- Possible bonuses

**Independent Dispatcher**:
- 5-10 trucks: $60,000-$100,000
- 20+ trucks: $100,000-$150,000

### Freight Broker Path

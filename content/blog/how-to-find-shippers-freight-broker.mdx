# 15 Proven Ways to Find Shippers as a Freight Broker

**Finding shippers is the lifeblood of your freight brokerage.** This guide reveals exactly how successful brokers find, qualify, and land shippers consistently.

## The Numbers Game Reality

### Industry Averages
- **Cold calls to conversation**: 10:1
- **Conversations to quote**: 3:1
- **Quotes to customer**: 5:1
- **Calls to new shipper**: 150:1

This means 150 calls = 15 conversations = 5 quotes = 1 new shipper

## Strategy 1: Cold Calling (Still King)

### Where to Find Numbers
1. **Industry directories**
2. **Google Maps searches**
3. **LinkedIn Sales Navigator**
4. **ZoomInfo/Seamless.ai**
5. **State manufacturing associations**

### Proven Cold Call Script
"Hi [Name], I'm Mike from LoadVoice Logistics. I'm calling because I specialize in helping [Industry] companies reduce shipping costs on [Specific Lane]. We just saved [Similar Company] 18% on their Texas shipments. Do you have 2 minutes to see if I could do something similar for you?"

### Daily Targets
- **Dials**: 100-150
- **Conversations**: 10-15
- **Quotes sent**: 3-5
- **Follow-ups**: 20-30

## Strategy 2: LinkedIn Mastery

### Profile Optimization
**Headline**: "Helping [Industry] Companies Save 20% on Freight | [Specialty] Expert"

**About Section Must Include**:
- Specific results you've achieved
- Industries you specialize in
- Problems you solve
- Social proof/testimonials

### Connection Strategy
1. Connect with 20 logistics managers daily
2. Send personalized messages
3. Share valuable content 3x/week
4. Comment on prospects' posts
5. Use LoadVoice success stories

### Message Template
"Hi [Name], I noticed you're handling logistics at [Company]. I specialize in [specific thing they ship] and recently helped [competitor] reduce detention charges by 40%. Would you be open to a brief call to share how?"

## Strategy 3: Load Board Mining

### The Reverse Strategy
1. Search posted loads on DAT/Truckstop
2. Note consistent shippers
3. Research the companies
4. Call with specific lane knowledge

### What to Say
"I noticed you regularly ship from Dallas to Chicago. I have dedicated capacity on that lane and can offer consistent pricing. Can we discuss a contract rate?"

## Qualifying Shippers

### Key Questions
1. "How many loads per month?"
2. "What lanes do you ship?"
3. "Current transportation spend?"
4. "Biggest shipping challenge?"
5. "Decision-making process?"

### Red Flags
- Less than 5 loads/month
- Only spot freight
- Unwilling to share information
- No budget authority
- Existing exclusive contracts

# A Real Day in the Life of a $150,000 Freight Broker

**Want to know what freight brokers actually do all day?** This hour-by-hour breakdown shows the reality of freight brokering, from early morning rate checks to evening relationship building.

## Meet Sarah: 3-Year Freight Broker

### Her Stats
- **Experience**: 3 years in brokerage
- **Monthly Loads**: 120-150
- **Annual Income**: $150,000+
- **Commission Rate**: 35% of gross profit
- **Average Day**: 10-12 hours
- **Work Location**: Home office

## 5:30 AM - The Early Bird Advantage

### Wake Up & Market Check (5:30-6:00 AM)
```
Coffee brewing...
Check overnight emails: 12 new
Review DAT spot rates
Scan FreightWaves headlines
Check fuel prices
Review today's load list
```

**Why So Early?**: "Carriers post trucks early. Being first to call at 6 AM gets best rates."

### Morning Prep (6:00-6:30 AM)
- Review 8 loads picking up today
- Check weather for delays
- Prioritize callback list
- Set daily revenue goal: $2,500
- Quick workout or walk

## 6:30 AM - The Golden Hours

### Carrier Calls Begin (6:30-8:00 AM)
**Activity Log**:
```
6:30 - Call carrier for Houston load
6:45 - Negotiate rate: $2,800→$2,650
7:00 - Confirm Chicago pickup
7:15 - Post 3 loads on DAT
7:30 - Check carrier references
7:45 - Book Phoenix load
8:00 - Send rate confirmations
```

**Calls Made**: 25
**Loads Booked**: 3
**Gross Profit**: $1,100

**Pro Tip**: "I use LoadVoice to record all morning negotiations. Saves me from note-taking."

## 8:00 AM - Customer Time

### Shipper Check-ins (8:00-9:30 AM)
**Customer Interactions**:
1. **ABC Manufacturing** - Weekly planning call
2. **XYZ Distributors** - Rate quote for new lane
3. **QRS Foods** - Service issue resolution
4. **New Prospect** - Discovery call

**Email Templates Used**:
- Good morning check-in
- Rate quote follow-up
- Capacity update
- Service recovery

**Revenue Opportunity**: $15,000 in quotes sent

## 9:30 AM - Operations Management

### Load Tracking & Problem Solving (9:30-11:00 AM)
**Real Issues Handled**:
```
9:30 - Driver delayed 2 hours (weather)
9:45 - Call shipper, manage expectations
10:00 - Detention dispute resolution
10:15 - Find recovery load for carrier
10:30 - Update 12 loads in TMS
10:45 - Process carrier paperwork
```

**Crisis Averted**: Saved $400 detention charge through negotiation

### Team Meeting (11:00-11:30 AM)
- Review team performance
- Share market intelligence
- Discuss problem accounts
- Celebrate wins (Jennifer booked 10 loads!)

## 11:30 AM - Sales Push

### New Business Development (11:30 AM-1:00 PM)
**Cold Calling Session**:
```
Dials: 40
Connections: 6
Conversations: 4
Quotes Requested: 2
Follow-ups Scheduled: 3
```

**Script That Worked Today**:
"Hi John, I noticed you ship regular LTL to Dallas. I have a consolidation program that saved a similar company 22% last month. Worth a quick discussion?"

### Lunch "Break" (1:00-1:30 PM)
- Eating at desk while checking emails
- Reviewing afternoon priorities
- Quick market update check
- Respond to urgent texts

**Reality**: "Haven't taken a real lunch break in 2 years"

## 1:30 PM - The Afternoon Grind

### Rate Negotiations & Bookings (1:30-3:30 PM)
**Actual Negotiations**:

**Load 1**: Dallas to Chicago
- Shipper rate: $3,200
- Initial carrier ask: $3,000

- **Margin**: $450 (14%)

**Load 2**: LA to Phoenix
- Shipper rate: $1,800
- Carrier rate: $1,500
- **Margin**: $300 (17%)

**Loads Booked**: 5
**Gross Profit**: $1,875

### Administrative Tasks (3:30-4:30 PM)

- Carrier packet collection
- Insurance verification
- Credit checks

- CRM updates

**Tools Used**:

## 4:30 PM - End of Day Push

### Final Coverage Push (4:30-6:00 PM)
**Urgent Loads to Cover**:
1. Tomorrow morning pickup - Atlanta
2. Hot shot to Detroit - Tonight
3. Team driver needed - Cross-country

**Success Rate**: 2 of 3 covered
**Challenge**: Hot shot required $500 premium

### Daily Wrap-up (6:00-6:30 PM)
- Update customer on tomorrow's loads
- Final email check
- Set tomorrow's priorities
- Review today's numbers

**Today's Performance**:
```
Loads Booked: 11
Gross Profit: $3,850
My Commission (35%): $1,347
Calls Made: 125
Emails Sent: 67
Miles Walked (standing desk): 2.3
```

## 6:30 PM - Never Really Off

### Evening Activities (6:30-9:00 PM)
- Dinner with family (phone nearby)
- Check critical emails
- Respond to West Coast customers
- Take carrier call about morning pickup
- Review tomorrow's hot loads

**The Reality**: "I'm never truly off. A $5,000 load opportunity doesn't care about dinner time."

### Before Bed (9:00-10:00 PM)
- Set alarm for 5:30 AM
- Final email check
- Review tomorrow's calendar
- Check weather for load routes
- Prep clothes for tomorrow (casual Friday!)

## Different Broker Schedules

### Corporate Broker (C.H. Robinson)
- 8 AM - 5 PM schedule
- Salary + small commission
- Less flexibility
- More support
- Better work-life balance

### Agency Broker (Landstar Agent)
- Complete flexibility
- 100% commission
- Work from anywhere
- No support
- Extreme variation

### Digital Broker (Convoy/Uber Freight)
- Tech-heavy role
- Regular hours
- Salary-based
- Less relationship
- More data analysis

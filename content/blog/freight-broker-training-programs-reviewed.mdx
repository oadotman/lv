## Detailed Program Reviews

### 1. Freight Broker Boot Camp - Best Overall
**Cost**: $1,995 (payment plans available)
**Duration**: 3 weeks intensive
**Format**: 100% online, self-paced with live sessions

**What You Learn**:
- Industry fundamentals
- Sales techniques
- Rate negotiation
- Carrier vetting
- Legal compliance
- Business development

**Included Materials**:
- Lifetime access to materials
- Sample contracts
- Rate calculation tools
- Carrier agreement templates
- 90 days of mentorship

**Pros**:
✅ Highest job placement rate
✅ Excellent instructor support
✅ Real-world scenarios
✅ Money-back guarantee

**Cons**:
❌ Fast-paced (not for everyone)
❌ Limited networking opportunities
❌ No TMS hands-on training

**Graduate Review**:
"Started earning commissions within 6 weeks of completing the program. The sales training alone was worth the investment." - Jennifer K., now earning $85,000/year

### 2. Brooke Transportation Training
**Cost**: $2,495
**Duration**: 6 weeks
**Format**: Online with weekly live sessions

**Unique Features**:
- 1-on-1 mentorship included
- Actual load booking practice
- TMS software training
- Business plan development

**Best For**: Those wanting to start their own brokerage

### 3. Total Quality Logistics (TQL) Training
**Cost**: Free (if hired)
**Duration**: 8 weeks
**Location**: Cincinnati, OH

**The Deal**:
- Paid training ($40,000 base salary during training)
- Must commit to 1 year
- Intensive sales focus
- Real customer interaction

**Reality Check**:
- Very competitive environment
- 60+ hour work weeks common
- High burnout rate (40% leave within year 1)
- But top performers make $150,000+

### 4. Community College Programs

**Typical Program**:
- Cost: $500-$1,500
- Duration: 8-12 weeks
- Format: Evening/weekend classes

**Examples**:
- Houston Community College: $650
- Broward College (Florida): $795
- Cerritos College (California): $550

**Pros**: Affordable, structured learning
**Cons**: Slower pace, less industry connection
